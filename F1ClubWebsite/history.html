History of F1 in Ireland

Ireland and F1

The origins of F1's popularity in Ireland can be traced back to the 90s, where Jordan GP was a successful team run by Irishman, Eddie Jordan. In 1999, they achieved most success with one of their drivers called Heinz-Harald Frentzen who nearly won the championship that year.

Revival in recent years

After Jordan GP left F1 at the end of 2005, Interest in F1 here slowly died down until 2019. The Netflix seires "Drive to Survive" was released and the success of it grew interest back in F1. Additionally, the 2021 F1 season brought in millions of viewers, due to the championship battle between Lewis Hamilton and Max Verstappen.

Where F1 stands now

F1 is now one of the most watched motorsports (or even sports) today. Even though the past few seasons may be boring to some (with Max winning often), there's no doubt that F1 in Ireland has a loyal audience. We hope that this club can bring all the fans here together.
